Phat Diem Stone Cathedral – Kenh Ga Floating Village - Vietnam Packages

Marvel at Phat Diem Stone Cathedral: Phat Diem Cathedral in the northern province of Ninh Binh is not just any old church. It is one of the most intriguing religious buildings in the country. The architect, Tran Luc, was a local Catholic who had no formal training. His vision, erected in the late 19th century, is a fantastic mix of East & West.
The Village of Kenh Ga (Chicken canal) gets its name, apparently, from the number of wild chickens that used to live here. That is the riverine way of life and the main draw. The local people seem to spend most of their lives on the water: watching over their floating fish-breeding pens, harvesting river grass for fish feed or selling veggies boat-to-boat. Even the children commute to school by boat. The river used for everything from bathing, to washing plucked chickens. In particularly, here people row boats with their feet.

Tour schedule

Morning:
8:30: Drive to Phat Diem, go for a walk around the cathedral to see the most harmonious combination of architecture between the east and the west – Roman, Gothic, and Pagoda. After that we walk around to discover daily life of local people.
12:30 pm: Have lunch at hotel.
Afternoon:
14:00 pm: Departure to Kenh Ga, have a motor boat trip along Hoang Long river – King Dragon river to enjoy river and rice paddy sceneries, and to see how the local people's lives are in the mountainous area – inland Ha Long bay. Walk about 2 km among rice fields to admire a wonderfully natural cave with many stalactites and stalagmites shaped miraculously.
