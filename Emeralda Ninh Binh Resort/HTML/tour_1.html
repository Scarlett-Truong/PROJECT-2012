Kenh Ga floating village - Bai Dinh Pagoda - Vietnam Packages

The Village of Kenh Ga (Chicken canal) gets its name, apparently, from the number of wild chickens that used to live here. That is the riverine way of life and the main draw. The local people seem to spend most of their lives on the water: watching over their floating fish-breeding pens, harvesting river grass for fish feed or selling veggies boat-to-boat. Even the children commute to school by boat. The river used for everything from bathing, to washing plucked chickens, Particularly, here people row boats with their feet.
Bai Dinh Pagoda or Bai Dinh Spiritual and Cultural Complex is a complex of Buddhist temples in Bai Dinh Mountain, consisting of one old temple area and a new temple area. This is considered the largest complex of Buddhist temples at completion in Vietnam. This is a popular site for Buddhist pilgrims across Vietnam. Currently, this mass is executing many items of Pagoda including Tam The (Past, Present and Future) Temple, Sanctuary and Bodhisattva Kwan Yin statue, Dharma Temple, Bell-tower, Three-door temple gate, and dormitory area for Buddhist monks, etc.
